Move Beyond Demos. Ship Production Systems.

Minor / no change
US Brochure · Original

Agentic AI has moved past the prototype. Companies don't need another chatbot demo. They need engineers who can scope a problem with a customer, build a production-grade agentic system, deploy it inside the customer's environment, and own it end to end.

The Forward Deployed Engineer (FDE) sits at the intersection of software engineering, customer-facing delivery, and AI deployment. The FDE owns production code that runs inside the customer's environment, covering orchestration, safety, evaluation, cost control, and operations.

This program is not about “using AI.” It trains the engineering judgment senior AI roles are hired for: deciding when to use agents versus deterministic systems, selecting the right design patterns, and justifying architectural trade-offs.

It then extends that depth with the distinctly FDE craft: customer discovery, problem framing, scoping & commercials, enterprise wiring & governance, operations & customer handover, and closes with FDE-specific interview preparation.

Recommended Additions Beyond the US Brochure

Gaps found by comparing the US brochure against industry-standard bootcamp/upskilling collateral and this program's own market research. None of this is built into the brochure yet — each item needs a decision first.

1. Named Alumni Success Stories

Zero individual testimonials or case studies currently appear (e.g., “X, a backend engineer, is now an FDE at Y”). Specific, named stories consistently outperform aggregate stats for credibility — aggregate numbers are easy to dismiss as marketing.

2. A Condensed FAQ in the Brochure Itself

The India landing page carries an FAQ; the brochure carries none, forcing a reader back to the website for basic questions. A 5–6 question FAQ page here would reduce avoidable advisor-call volume.

3. Separating Instructor Pedigree from Hiring Evidence

“Instructors & Alumni From” currently blends “companies our instructors work at” with implied hiring outcomes. Worth separating explicitly once FDE-specific hiring-partner data exists, so the two claims don't read as one.

4. A Short “Day in the Life of an FDE” Narrative

Helps prospects self-select before enrolling. Competitor content (Scaler, fde.academy) leans on this narrative device; IK's brochure is currently all curriculum-and-outcomes, no lived-experience framing.
